Before You Use This Tool

This is for individual screening, workplace health education, and guided action planning.

Not a diagnosis

Purpose. This tool helps you reflect on burnout-related symptoms across personal, work-related, and client or patient-related domains. It is designed for personal use and workplace support discussion.

Important limitation. It does not diagnose burnout, depression, anxiety, or any psychiatric condition. WHO describes burnout as an occupational phenomenon linked to chronic workplace stress that has not been successfully managed.

Safety first. If you have thoughts of self-harm, severe distress, panic symptoms, major functional impairment, workplace violence, bullying, harassment, or traumatic exposure, seek prompt help from a qualified healthcare professional, nearest Emergency Department, Klinik Kesihatan, occupational health service, or local emergency services.

References

References used to structure the screening explanation, scoring, and workplace action planning. Accessed 25 June 2026.

  1. World Health Organization. Burn-out an occupational phenomenon: ICD-11. WHO states burnout is included as an occupational phenomenon and is not classified as a medical condition.
  2. Ministry of Health Malaysia self-test page. Copenhagen Burnout Inventory (CBI). The page lists CBI as a self-test for identifying magnitude and types of burnout among staff.
  3. Department of Occupational Safety and Health Malaysia. Guidelines on Psychosocial Risk Assessment and Management at the Workplace 2024. Published 4 November 2024.
  4. World Health Organization. Mental health at work. Includes organisational interventions, manager training, worker training, reasonable accommodation, and return-to-work support.
  5. National Institute for Health and Care Excellence. NG212 Mental wellbeing at work: recommendations. Covers prevention, supportive work environments, manager involvement, communication, and organisational approaches.
  6. Kristensen TS, Borritz M, Villadsen E, Christensen KB. The Copenhagen Burnout Inventory: A new tool for the assessment of burnout. National Research Centre for the Working Environment. The CBI uses personal, work-related, and client-related burnout scales; the family/friends energy item is reverse scored.
  7. International Organization for Standardization. ISO 45003:2021 Psychological health and safety at work. Provides guidance for managing psychosocial risk within an occupational health and safety management system.
  8. World Health Organization and International Labour Organization. Mental health at work: policy brief. Provides practical actions for prevention of psychosocial risks, promotion of mental health, and support for workers.

This tool is an individual support aid. It should complement, not replace, employer psychosocial risk assessment processes, occupational health advice, or professional healthcare assessment.
